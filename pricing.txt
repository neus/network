# NEUS Pricing — Trust Receipt Credits and Plans

# NEUS is credits-based. Start free, then scale checks and saved receipts as your product grows.
# Hosted sign-in (intent=login) is included. Credits apply when a trust check runs or a receipt is saved (gates, API, MCP), not when someone signs in.
# Reading a receipt at a gate costs about one credit. Saving a new receipt costs a few credits depending on the check.
# Rate: $0.0025 per credit (USD). Prepaid credit packs available: 500, 1000, 2000, 5000, 10000.
# Full plan comparison, credit packs, and the live estimator: https://neus.network/pricing

## Basic (Free)
- Price: $0 / month
- Included: 500 credits / month
- Features: Hosted sign-in and trust checks, reusable receipts, credit top-ups when checks outgrow the free pool
- Payment: card (self-serve), no charge on free tier

## Pro
- Price: $19.99 / month (billed monthly) · $199.90 / year (billed annually — 2 months free)
- Included: 50,000 credits / month
- Features: Everything in Basic, plus agent runs with identity/scoped permission/receipts, higher API throughput, priority support queue, Pro badge, private Discord channel, preview of upcoming features
- Payment: card (self-serve)

## Enterprise
- Price: Custom — contact for contract pricing
- Included: 500,000+ credits / month (custom packages available)
- Features: Branded verification, rollout support, custom credit packages, SLAs, security review path, flexible billing (card/invoice/procurement), pilot programs with onboarding credits
- Contact: https://neus.network/contact?topic=Enterprise

## x402 — Pay-per-call
- When credits cannot cover a request, NEUS returns HTTP 402 with a signed quote.
- Pay (USDC or native ETH via POST /api/v1/payments/verify) and retry the same request with the receipt in the PAYMENT-SIGNATURE header.
- Card (Stripe) is not used for x402; it is available for gate checkout only.

## Credit costs (approximate)
- Reading a receipt at a gate: ~1 credit
- Saving a new receipt: a few credits (varies by check)
- Human verification, wallet risk, and agent permission checks use more credits than a simple ownership check.
