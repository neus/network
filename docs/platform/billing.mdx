Complete [Get started](../get-started) first (link your app, credits, `appId`). This page explains **who is charged** for verification and related API usage.

## What costs credits

Metered actions such as verification submissions and gate checks consume credits on a **payer wallet** NEUS resolves from the request (see [How the payer is chosen](#how-the-payer-is-chosen)). Credits are separate from proof visibility (public, unlisted, or private).

## `appId` and billing

Set `appId` on `NeusClient` to send `X-Neus-App`. That **attributes** traffic to your app for analytics and support—it does **not** choose who pays. Billing follows the signing wallet, sponsor grants, configured gate payer rules, or x402—not the app label alone.

## Who pays: apps vs individuals

| You are building… | Typical payer | What to configure |
|-------------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **A product or team** | Your org wallet (credits you fund) | [Get started](../get-started): link app, keep credits on the org wallet, use **sponsor grants** so billable calls debit your balance instead of the end user’s |
| **Personal use** | Your own wallet | Hosted Verify or SDK with your wallet; your NEUS credits apply, or use **x402** when you see `402 Payment Required` |

Same SDK and API—the difference is **which wallet** NEUS treats as the payer for that request.

## How the payer is chosen

NEUS evaluates payer context in this order:

1. **Paid receipt** — x402 or other settled payment attached to the request, when applicable  
2. **Sponsor grant** — valid `X-Sponsor-Grant`: charges the org that issued the grant  
3. **Gate owner** — when a gate is configured so the gate owner pays  
4. **Signing wallet** — the wallet that signed the verification or check  
5. **x402 path** — payment required flow (`402` → pay → retry with `PAYMENT-SIGNATURE`)  
6. **Otherwise** — limits, `402`, or other guards may apply  

You cannot move billing by putting `appId` in a JSON body alone. Payer resolution uses signatures, grants, and session mechanics—not the app label by itself.

## Recommended setup for app builders

1. Create and configure your app in the platform.  
2. Keep credits on the **organization wallet** that should pay.  
3. Issue short-lived **sponsor grant** tokens and pass them on the client: `new NeusClient({ appId: '…', sponsorGrant: token })`.  
4. Every billable call is **labeled** with `appId` and **debited** under the grant when it is valid.  

<Warning>
Anonymous or unattributed traffic is not a substitute for production billing. Heavy verification or gate volume without a clear payer context may hit different limits than sponsored traffic.
</Warning>

## Individuals

1. Use [Hosted Verify](../cookbook/auth-hosted-verify) or the SDK with your wallet.  
2. Credits come from your NEUS balance on that wallet.  
3. If you receive `402`, complete x402 payment and retry with `PAYMENT-SIGNATURE` (see [x402](./x402)).  

## SDK fields that affect billing

| Field | Role |
|--------|------|
| `appId` | Sets `X-Neus-App` for **attribution** (not payer selection by itself) |
| `sponsorGrant` | Sets `X-Sponsor-Grant` so your org pays when the grant is valid |
| `paymentSignature` | Carries `PAYMENT-SIGNATURE` for x402 retries after payment |

The SDK does not auto-pay x402 invoices. Your app implements `402` → pay → retry, or you keep org credits sufficient to avoid `402`.

## Visibility vs billing

Public or unlisted proofs can be **read** without sign-in where the API allows. **Private** visibility is an access rule; it does not replace payer rules above.
