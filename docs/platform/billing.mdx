Onboarding: [Get started](../get-started). Here: **payer order**, sponsors, **x402**.

**Shipped products:** org pays (credits + sponsor grants). Users aren’t asked to fund credits first. Self-pay + x402 → [x402](./x402).

## Charges

**`appId`** labels traffic to your app (**attribution**). It does **not** choose the payer by itself.

Who pays: sponsor grant (best for customers) → signing wallet → x402 → limits. No sponsor → signer may pay—fine for dev; use sponsors for customer traffic.

## Who you are

|  | Billing |
|--|---------|
| **Product** | You pay: sponsors + org credits. Users see hosted verify / your UI. |
| **Solo** | Your credits (or x402). `appId` optional. |

Same SDK/API—different funder.

## You pay for users

1. App + org credits.
2. Short-lived **sponsor grant** so usage hits **your** balance.
3. `new NeusClient({ appId, sponsorGrant })` so both values are sent on SDK calls.

[Platform setup](./setup)

<Warning>
Heavy anonymous checks without a payer ≠ production. No sponsor / no session → dev or tiny volume only.
</Warning>

## Individuals

1. Hosted verify or SDK + your wallet.
2. Your NEUS balance.
3. HTTP 402 → x402 + `PAYMENT-SIGNATURE` → [x402](./x402)

## Payer order

Paid receipt → sponsor grant → gate owner (if set) → signing wallet → x402 → payment required.

Not: “bill this `appId`” in the body—payer comes from session/crypto.

## SDK

| Field | Role |
|-------|------|
| `appId` | App attribution on requests |
| `sponsorGrant` | Bill your org for this session |
| `paymentSignature` | Reply after an x402 payment challenge |

SDK doesn’t auto-pay x402—you retry after payment or keep credits healthy.

## Visibility vs billing

Public / unlisted: eligibility without login. Private: visibility rules—not the same as who pays.
