Start with **[Get started](../get-started)** for the setup checklist. This page covers billing details, edge cases, and x402.

## Why this can feel confusing

**`appId` on `NeusClient`** sends **`X-Neus-App`** so NEUS can **attribute** usage to your product. It does **not**, by itself, move the bill to your NEUS account balance.

**Credits are charged to a *payer wallet*** resolved from how the request is authenticated: the **signing wallet**, a **sponsor grant** (your org), an **x402 receipt**, or certain **gate** setups. Until you add one of those, **`appId` alone does not move billing** — end users can still need their own credits or another payer context.

---

## Apps vs individuals

| You are… | Priority | Billing |
|----------|------------------------|------------------------|
| **Building an app** | Users should not hit random “out of credits” walls; **you** want predictable cost | **You pay** for metered verification and checks via **sponsor grants** (and **credits on your org wallet** in the platform). Users only see hosted verify / your UI. |
| **Using NEUS for yourself** | Your own proofs and profile | **You pay** with **your** NEUS credits (or x402 when you run out). **`appId`** is optional (attribution only). |

Same SDK and API — different **who funds credits**.

---

## Recommended path for **app integrators**

1. **Platform** — Create/configure your **app**, keep **credits** on the **organization wallet** you use for that app (the app owner).
2. **Sponsor users** — Issue short-lived **`X-Sponsor-Grant`** tokens so billable calls debit **your** org balance (capped by the grant), not the end user’s.
3. **SDK** — `new NeusClient({ appId: '…', sponsorGrant: token })` so every request is **labeled** and **paid** under your grant.

Details and grant flow: **[Partner setup](../cookbook/hub-integrator)**.

<Warning>
Do not rely on anonymous traffic for heavy **gate checks** or verification volume — without a payer context, behavior and limits are not the same as production **sponsored** traffic. Requests with **no sponsor, no signature, and no receipt** are appropriate for **development or very low volume** only.
</Warning>

---

## Recommended path for **individuals**

1. Use **hosted verify** or **SDK** with your own wallet.
2. **Credits** come from **your** NEUS balance on that wallet.
3. If you hit **402**, use **x402** (pay and retry with **`PAYMENT-SIGNATURE`**) — see **[x402](./x402)**.

---

## How NEUS selects the payer

Precedence: **paid receipt** → **sponsor grant** → **gate owner pays** (if configured) → **wallet that signed the request** → **x402 paywall path** → otherwise you may hit **limits** or **payment required**.

You do not send “bill this `appId`” in the body and expect the wallet to switch — **payer is always derived from crypto/session mechanics**, not from `appId` alone.

---

## SDK configuration

| Config | Effect |
|--------|--------|
| `appId` | Sets **`X-Neus-App`** — **analytics / attribution** |
| `sponsorGrant` | Sets **`X-Sponsor-Grant`** — **your org pays** (when grant is valid) |
| `paymentSignature` | Sets **`PAYMENT-SIGNATURE`** — **retry after x402** |

The SDK does **not** auto-complete x402 payments; your app handles **402 → pay → retry** (or you avoid 402 by keeping org credits healthy).

---

## Visibility vs billing

- **Public / unlisted proofs** — eligibility checks can work without the user signing in.
- **Private** — visibility rules still apply; **billing** is separate.

### When the app owner pays

**“App owner pays everything”** is achieved with **sponsor grants and org credits**, not with `appId` alone. A future protocol change could add a single switch so a linked app’s owner wallet becomes the default payer for server-side keys.
